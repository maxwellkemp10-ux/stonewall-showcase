# Stonewall Showcase

> Stonewall is a knowledge-control system for complex, high-consequence work.

> ℹ️ Built for teams that cannot afford drift, delay, or fragmented operating memory.

### Executive premise

Most organizations do not suffer from a lack of information.

They suffer from weak control over information.

Critical knowledge becomes fragmented.

Ownership becomes diffuse.

Search replaces structure.

Rework replaces continuity.

> ✅ Stonewall is designed for live use, not archive storage.

### Strategic thesis

Stonewall converts knowledge from a passive repository into an active operating asset.

It introduces structure where most systems tolerate sprawl.

That shift improves speed, consistency, and execution quality.

### What it does

* Consolidates fragmented inputs into one governed system.
* Preserves chronology, naming, and source integrity.
* Routes work to the correct decision surface.
* Reduces rework across drafting, review, and maintenance cycles.
* Provides a purpose-built CLI for corpus stats, full-text search, and health diagnostics.
* Automates corpus hardening with PDF-to-Markdown sidecar generation and OCR fallback repair.
* Runs AI-powered QC sweeps that flag missing dates, unlinked records, and duplicate entries.

### Design principles

* Single source before synthesis
* Routing before drafting
* Rules before exceptions
* Precision before polish
* Reuse before reinvention
* Maintenance as infrastructure, not cleanup

### Business value

Stonewall reduces the hidden tax of fragmented knowledge.

It improves execution quality without increasing complexity.

It raises consistency across long time horizons.

It creates a durable operating advantage in information-heavy environments.

### Risk controls
