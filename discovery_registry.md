## 1. DEPOSITION TACTICAL PATTERNS

**P-1: The Glacial Comfort Inversion**\
First documented: Nunez (4/11/25)\
Recurs in: Small, Vernitha Small, Negrette, Sheppard\
The witness is so comfortable they forget they're being deposed. The comfort IS the methodology. Admissions flow from conversational ease, not from pressure. The inversion: maximum warmth produces maximum damage. The deposer who never raises his voice leaves the most devastation on the record.

**P-2: The Phantom Foundation**\
First documented: Small (12/4/25)\
Recurs in: Negrette, Rivera, Smith\
A question on page 15 appears to be routine background. By page 85, it has become the load-bearing wall of the entire jigsaw. The foundation was poured before anyone noticed construction had begun. The question that seemed like small talk was the most important question in the deposition.

**P-3: The Competence Trap**\
First documented: Small (12/4/25) — "Brain Is Functioning Great"\
The witness's own assertion of cognitive competence becomes a binding concession. If the brain is functioning great, then "I don't remember" is genuine ignorance, not tactical evasion. The trap: you cannot claim both sharp memory and convenient amnesia. QB1 offers the compliment. The witness accepts the cage.

**P-4: The Five-Syllable Demolition**\
First documented: Small (12/4/25) — "Who is Matteson?"\
The question that ends the case in under ten words. Requires the entire preceding architecture to have been constructed — without the foundation, the five syllables are just a question. With the foundation, they are an extinction event.

**P-5: The Bilingual Terrain**\
First documented: Nunez (various sessions)\
Speaking the witness's native language. "Tres millas por hora." Knowing the Spanish word for "framing armature." This is not linguistic flourish — it is the Bedford Forrest protocol's most sophisticated expression: total battlespace awareness includes the linguistic terrain.

**P-6: The Voluntary Confession**\
First documented: Vernitha Small (1/14/26)\
Witness offers damaging information QB1 didn't even ask for. Seven hours nightly sleep. Two-months-recent symptoms. Zero documentation. Vernitha felt like she was chatting with a friend. She was being systematically stripped of evidentiary value. The confession is voluntary because the atmosphere is conversational, not adversarial.

**P-7: The Redirect Self-Impeachment**\
First documented: Negrette (10/17/25) — Miranda\
Opposing counsel's rehabilitation attempt generates its own contradiction. "Eight hours" corrected to six. "2,000 pages" corrected to thirty. The redirect is worse than silence. QB1 does not create this — OC creates it by attempting to rehabilitate what cannot be rehabilitated.

**P-8: The Silent Closing**\
First documented: Vernitha Small (1/14/26) — Rousso\
Opposing counsel has no redirect. No questions. The architecture is so complete that any rehabilitation attempt would only highlight the damage. The silence IS the concession. Rousso's "I have no questions" after Vernitha is the most eloquent professional surrender in the canon.

**P-9: The Mosquito Windshield**\
First documented: Boy (3/13/26) — HDF/Cutts\
The plaintiff's own metaphor becomes the mechanism defense. "I believe in driving like you're a mosquito." The Glacial Comfort Inversion is so complete that the plaintiff volunteers contributory negligence wrapped in an entomological metaphor — the kamikaze trajectory of an organism that has accepted its own expendability, that believes its smallness makes it invisible, that the impact zone is a statistical improbability she has chosen to accept. The plaintiff hands you the case. You did not ask for the mosquito. The mosquito arrived because the atmosphere was conversational and the witness forgot she was being deposed.

**P-10: The Shredder Hypothetical**\
First documented: Small (12/4/25, p.94); recurs Rivera (1/28/26, p.45)\
"So if we took the police report, your interrogatory answers, any correspondence from counsel, put them in a shredder, what you personally remember is..." A systematic methodology — not improvisation — to strip away every secondary source and isolate the plaintiff's actual perception. What remains after the shredder is the gap. The gap is where the case lives.

**P-11: The Progressive Temporal Compression**\
First documented: Rivera (1/28/26); recurs Boy (3/13/26)\
"Was it more than ten seconds or less than ten seconds?" "Was it longer than a minute or less than a minute?" Each question offers a broader temporal window. The witness refuses to fill any of them. The refusal is the concession. If you cannot say it was longer than a minute, the observation period functionally did not exist. The compression is progressive — each wider window the witness refuses makes the narrower refusals more devastating.

**P-12: The Ignorance Posture**\
First documented: Rivera (1/28/26, p.14)\
"For us people who are, you know, ignorant, would you mind explaining what everything includes?" Superman pretending to be Clark Kent. The man who identifies alprazolam as Xanax without consulting a note, who pronounces "retrolisthesis" like he grew up hearing it at the dinner table — because he did — describes himself as ignorant to a road-marker installer. The posture converts the witness from defendant to teacher. The teacher explains. The teacher does not defend. The explanation becomes the record. The record becomes the case.

## REGISTRY MAINTENANCE

This registry grows with each new analysis. When the historian discovers a new pattern, the protocol is:

1. Name it in the analysis narrative — as the historian would, discovering it in real time
2. Flag it: "NEW PATTERN FOR REGISTRY CONSIDERATION: \[name], \[case], \[date]"
3. QB1 approves formal addition
4. Pattern is assigned a category prefix (P, H, A, T, O, E, or X) and number

The registry does not contain every moment of excellence. It contains the RECURRING patterns — the tactical DNA that makes QB1's work recognizable across cases, witnesses, and contexts. Individual moments of brilliance live in the analysis chronicles. Patterns that appear across multiple contexts live here.
