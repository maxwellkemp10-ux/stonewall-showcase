## Chapter IX: Johnny Got His Gun

### *In Which the Captain Runs Every File While the Factory Horns Its Corn, the Alliance Deepens Over Baudrillard and Beer-League Hockey, Little Satan Achieves Independent Flight, and Mr. Bone Discovers That This Particular Defense Firm Does Not Fold*

**Covering: March 2–6, 2026 | The BONEMAXXED Week, Concluded**

---

There is a particular quality of light that falls across the ATS UPS Team Chat in the first week of March 2026 — a kind of late-winter Tampa luminescence that illuminates, with forensic precision, the simultaneous existence of two entirely separate law firms operating from the same fourth-floor suite on West Kennedy Boulevard. The first firm is run by one man. It files motions at midnight, ghost-writes witness lists at 11:49 PM for allied counsel to file at 3:42 AM, confers with opposing counsel on contested CME deadlines, manages expert retention across three separate cases, drafts competing orders for Judge Oster, approves filings in real time from the Teams chat, and does all of this while fielding RADAR ALERTs about cases he has already addressed, answering questions about hearings nobody was told to set, and explaining to his own paralegal that nobody put through a phone call from the defense expert who was trying to reach him. The second firm occupies the same physical space but exists in a different dimension of professional reality entirely — one in which a .10 entry for "Releasing the Kraken" constitutes billable work, in which the phrase "I worked hard on those not taking away from anybody else's work...TEAM!!!!" follows the execution of a mail merge, and in which the concept of reading a file before acting upon it remains as foreign and exotic as the philosophy of Jean Baudrillard, which, as it happens, QB1 was explaining to his co-defendant counsel in a Winter Garden law office at roughly the same hour Big Satan was posting her BOOM GIF.

We begin, as the historian must, with the documentary record.

The ATS UPS Team Chat from the week of March 2–6, 2026, spans some thirty-odd screenshots across multiple PDF compilations, and what it reveals — when read with the meticulous attention QB1 demands and Big Satan has never once attempted — is a portrait of institutional life in which the man who passed the bar on his seventh attempt and then proceeded to run every file in the building is now so thoroughly embedded as the sole operational engine of the UPS vertical that even the corn horns have begun to orbit him like satellites orbiting the only gravitational body in the solar system.

Consider the sequence that opens the week. On Monday, March 2, Jennifer Escobar — Little Satan, though by this point in the chronicle the designation feels increasingly unfair to a woman who is, against all odds and institutional headwinds, beginning to function — asks the UPS Team Chat a straightforward question at 10:58 AM: "Did we ever get ID out in Egan so i can file NOD's." It is a good question. It is the kind of question that demonstrates awareness of a filing dependency — you cannot file Notices of Deposition until the Initial Disclosures have gone out, and the Initial Disclosures have been sitting in a holding pattern because the attorneys haven't approved them. Two minutes later, at 11:00 AM, Yisenia Toledo — Big Satan — quotes Jennifer's message back, slaps an **IMPORTANT** tag on it, and adds: "CCA/MAX: I've been waiting for it to be approved to file so we can release Subpoenas as well."

Now, the historian must pause here to appreciate the architecture of this particular corn horn, because it is a specimen of such refined kabuki craftsmanship that it deserves its own display case. Big Satan has taken Little Satan's organic, case-aware question and *re-presented it* — quoted back, IMPORTANT-tagged, reframed — as though Big Satan herself had been the one tracking this dependency all along. "I've been waiting for it to be approved." The possessive construction implies ownership. The word "waiting" implies diligence held in abeyance by forces beyond her control. The phrase "to file so we can release Subpoenas as well" implies a multi-step strategic vision in which the Initial Disclosures are merely the first domino in Big Satan's grand operational sequence. But the question originated with Little Satan. Big Satan merely quoted it back, added an exclamation point, and claimed the dependency as her own. This is Pattern #16 — the Corn Horn — operating at its purest frequency: the appropriation of another person's work product or initiative through the mechanism of the quoteback, the IMPORTANT tag, and the subtle grammatical reframing that transforms "I noticed this" into "I've been working on this."

Jennifer Escobar, to her enormous credit, responds at 11:00 AM with the quiet dignity of a woman who has just watched her question get swallowed whole: "thanks for update."

Meanwhile, in a law office in Winter Garden, Florida, two men from New York were having a very different kind of conversation.

The "lilt in his voice." QB1 heard it — the tell, the micro-expression in vocal form, the moment when Mr. Bone, Ronald I. Mr. Bone heard what he wanted to hear — a young defense attorney cracking under the weight of a $500,000 hard-floor case with a June 2026 trial term — and what he was actually hearing was the voice of a man who had ghost-written thirty-eight slides for a standing ovation in Dallas, who had passed the bar on his seventh attempt with a 135, who had built the Safety Valve Doctrine from an out-of-body experience in front of Judge Wolf, and who was, at that very moment, preparing a six-page motion of such architectural precision that it would force Bone to submit competing orders before Judge Oster while simultaneously managing the CME timeline, the expert retention, the Campanelli scheduling, the Spanish-speaking plaintiff's interpreter coordination, and the three-party logistics of a board-certified neurosurgeon's calendar.
