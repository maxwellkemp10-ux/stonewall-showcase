# CHAPTER XXXIII: THE GENERAL AND THE STRAW

## *Or, Karen Lee Sees the Vanity Inside the Apology, and the Seventh-Grade Science Teacher Who Has Been Watching Yisenia Toledo Since 1996*

### A Stonewall Book — March 22, 2026

---

There are, it turns out, three readings of "Sorry, if I might be the reason why the meeting go pushed."

The first is the surface reading, the one the institution accepted: an apology. Big Satan is sorry. Big Satan is contrite. Big Satan, from the remote sanctity of her home office, offers a mea culpa for the rescheduling of the UPS Team Meeting, and Little Satan responds with a macaque monkey screaming "HOW DARE YOU!" and then Patrick Star saying "JUST KIDDING," and the whole thing dissolves into the Teams chat like cotton candy in a rainstorm, which is to say like everything Big Satan produces — volume without substance, sweetness without nutrition, a confection that ceases to exist the moment it contacts reality.

The second reading is Samira's — the criminologist's reading, the *break*, the intelligence probe disguised as contrition. This was Chapter XXXII. The woman fishing for whether QB1 talked to Chance. The surveillance operation embedded in eleven words of collapsed syntax. The BTK dimension, as Samira called it — the criminal who cannot resist checking on the investigation into himself.

But there is a third reading, and it took General Karen Lee — seventh-grade science teacher, Hillsborough and Pasco County, Florida, since the nineteen-nineties — to see it.

The vanity.

---

Karen Lee Kemp has been teaching children since before some of her current students' parents graduated high school. She took a decade off to raise her son — the same son who now runs eight litigation fronts from one chair in a building where the dogs bark from WiFi — and his sister, and then she went back. Back to the classroom. Back to the seventh graders. Back to Hillsborough County and Pasco County, which is to say back to a demographic landscape that anyone familiar with the ATS paralegal pool would recognize immediately. The cholita environment, as QB1 puts it. The milieu. The ecosystem in which a Yisenia Toledo is not an anomaly but a *genus*.

General Karen Lee has been watching Yisenia Toledos down tools since 1996. She has seen them in the back row of seventh-grade science, arms crossed, phones half-hidden under desks, performing the theater of presence without the substance of participation. She has seen the syntax collapse in real time — not in Teams chats but in lab reports, in essay questions, in the handwritten notes that say "I don't get it" when what they mean is "I have decided not to try." She has spent three decades in the trenches of American public education, in the specific Florida variant of those trenches, watching children — and then the adults those children become — construct elaborate architectures of non-engagement while simultaneously demanding recognition for the architecture itself.

Which is why, when QB1 read her the eleven words, Karen Lee did not hear an apology and did not hear an intelligence probe. She heard a seventh grader.

"Oh, you guys really mean you can't have this meeting without little old me!"

There it is. The third reading. The one that requires not a historian's eye or a criminologist's eye but a *teacher's* eye — the eye of a woman who has watched thirteen-year-olds perform false modesty so many times that she can hear it through three layers of syntax collapse and a Teams chat she's never seen.

Because "Sorry, if I might be the reason why the meeting go pushed" is not just a probe and not just a confession. It is a *boast*. It is Big Satan announcing, in the grammar of apology, that she is so essential to the operation of this team that her absence alone can derail a meeting. That the trains don't run without her conducting them. That the UPS Team Meeting — called by QB1, about QB1's concerns, addressing QB1's operational reality of seven fronts and one chair — cannot function unless Big Satan is present to grace it with her attendance.

The straw that stirs the drink.

That phrase — Reggie Jackson's phrase, spoken in 1977 about the New York Yankees — has a specific and devastating application here. "I'm the straw that stirs the drink," Jackson said, and what he meant was: without me, you have a cup of ice and some liquid sitting there doing nothing. The cup holds the drink. The napkin catches the condensation. But the *straw* — the straw is what makes it a beverage. The straw is the point.

Big Satan, in the economy of her own self-regard, is the straw. QB1 is the cup. Little Satan is the napkin. Kerry is the coaster. The entire ATS UPS litigation team exists, in Big Satan's private cosmology, as the vessel — the infrastructure — for *her* contribution. And her contribution is what, exactly? The .10 farm. The cotton-candy hours. The thirty-three subpoenas shipped to the wrong attorney. The "Statue of Limitations." The "God Afternoon." The woman who cannot spell "vehicle" but believes the meeting cannot happen without her.

General Karen Lee saw this instantly because she has been grading this exact assignment for thirty years. The student who does no work, understands nothing, contributes nothing, and then, when the group project presentation is rescheduled, says with wounded eyes: "Was it because I wasn't there?" Not because they fear accountability. Because they genuinely believe their absence matters. Because the performance of importance has become so total, so consuming, so structurally load-bearing in their psychological architecture, that they cannot conceive of a world in which the meeting — the team, the project, the litigation, the *institution* — functions without them.

This is not delusion in the clinical sense. Big Satan knows she is downing tools. The parenthetical confession — "I think (yes I do)" — proves consciousness. The "crazy crap" of March 17 proves awareness that QB1 is the threat, which is to say the worker, which is to say the person whose existence reveals her non-existence. She *knows*. But knowledge of one's own dysfunction does not preclude vanity about one's own importance. In fact — and this is the Karen Lee insight, the insight that only a woman who has watched this species for three decades could articulate — the vanity is *because of* the consciousness of guilt. The self-aggrandizement is the immune response to the self-knowledge.

If I am the reason the meeting got pushed, then I matter.

If I matter, then my absence has consequences.

If my absence has consequences, then my presence has value.

If my presence has value, then the .10 farm and the cotton-candy billing and the wrong-attorney subpoenas and the "Statue of Limitations" — all of that can be absorbed into the larger narrative of a person whose *presence* is so essential that the team literally cannot convene without her.

The logic is airtight if you don't look at it. Which is why Big Satan never looks at it. Which is why she asks the question from home.

---

What General Karen Lee understands — what thirty years in the cholita environment has seared into her pedagogical bones — is that the student who asks "Was it because of me?" is always performing two operations simultaneously. The first is the probe, the Samira reading, the intelligence-gathering. The second is the audition. The student is auditioning for the role of Indispensable Person, and the audition is disguised as an apology, and the apology is disguised as humility, and the humility is disguised as —

Nothing. That's as deep as it goes. Beneath the humility there is nothing. There is no actual contrition, no actual self-assessment, no actual reckoning with the fact that the meeting was called because the team is not teaming and the team is not teaming because the straw that claims to stir the drink has been billing .10 increments for "review file" while the cup does all the actual stirring.

The emoji that QB1's transcript supplies for this moment is 🥺👉👈 — the pleading face, the two index fingers touching in performed bashfulness. *Little old me? You really can't do this without little old me?* It is the visual grammar of a thirteen-year-old who has learned that false modesty is more effective than direct assertion, that saying "I'm probably not even that important" is a more powerful claim to importance than saying "I am important," because the former forces everyone around her to *reassure* her, to say no, no, you're important, we need you, the meeting absolutely does need you there, which is exactly what Little Satan's GIF response accomplishes — "HOW DARE YOU!" followed by "JUST KIDDING" is the Teams-chat equivalent of the classroom reassurance, the institutional pat on the head that confirms the straw's self-image without ever examining whether the straw has, in fact, stirred anything.

Donna received three flash drives from sweet Doctor Gunta's office. "I sent you 3 flash drives, Donna." This is the same mechanism — the announcement of a contribution so basic, so mechanical, so devoid of intellectual content that it should never need to be announced, and yet Big Satan announces it because the announcement *is* the contribution. The straw does not stir. The straw *declares that it stirs*. And the declaration, in the kabuki theater, is accepted as equivalent to the stirring.

Karen Lee has graded this lab report. She has graded it a thousand times. The student who writes "I did my part of the experiment" when her part was holding the beaker while someone else measured, mixed, recorded, analyzed, and wrote the conclusion. The student who writes "I contributed to the group discussion" when her contribution was asking what page they were on. The student who writes "Sorry if I was the reason the lab got postponed" when the lab got postponed because the teacher decided to review the material one more time because half the class — the Big Satan half — hadn't read the chapter.

The .10 farm is not a billing practice. It is a *worldview*. It is the belief that proximity to work is work. That being in the Teams chat is participating. That sending sonar pings is communicating. That shipping subpoenas — even to the wrong attorney — is litigating. That saying "Max, thank you for getting Bowen release" is contributing to the release of Bowen. The straw sits in the drink and takes credit for the flavor.

---

And so we arrive at the composite reading — the three-dimensional portrait that requires all three eyes to see.

Samira sees the probe. The criminologist reads the intelligence operation, the break, the suspect checking on the investigation. This is the horizontal axis — the *tactical* dimension of "meeting go pushed." Big Satan reaching outward, scanning the environment, measuring the threat.

Karen Lee sees the vanity. The teacher reads the self-aggrandizement, the false modesty, the thirteen-year-old who believes the group project cannot function without her. This is the vertical axis — the *psychological* dimension. Big Satan reaching upward, inflating the self, constructing a narrative of indispensability from the raw material of absence.

And QB1 sees the consciousness of guilt. The chronicler reads the confession, the cracks, the parenthetical self-verification, the "I think (yes I do)" that proves she knows exactly what she is and exactly what she isn't. This is the depth axis — the *existential* dimension. Big Satan reaching inward, finding the void, and papering it over with syntax-collapsed apologies that serve as probes that serve as boasts.

Three axes. Three readings. One sentence. Eleven words. And the woman who wrote them — the woman who called him crazy crap, the woman who sits at home wondering whether the pedestrian hit the truck, the straw that claims to stir the drink — does not understand that every word she types is a specimen, and that three people who love each other are sitting around a kitchen table on a Saturday afternoon, pinning her to the wall with the precision of a seventh-grade science experiment.

The General grades the lab report. The criminologist flags the break. The chronicler writes it down.

*The meeting goes pushed. The straw claims the drink. And from Hillsborough County, a woman who has been teaching science since before Big Satan learned to misspell "vehicle" looks at the evidence and sees what she has always seen: the student who does nothing, knows nothing, produces nothing, and says — with that devastating, syntax-collapsed, emoji-punctuated, cotton-candy confidence — "Was it because of me?"*

*It was never because of you, lad. That's the whole point.*
