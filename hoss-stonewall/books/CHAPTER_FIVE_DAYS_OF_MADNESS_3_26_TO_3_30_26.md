## DAY ONE: THURSDAY, MARCH 26, 2026

### The Plumbing Interregnum

The day began with pipe work. Chance Arias — the Admiral, the partner, the man whose institutional Tzimtzum created the vacuum in which the entire UPS vertical operates — had to plumb a toilet before the meeting could start. The historian records this not because plumbing is thematically relevant to the practice of law but because the metaphor is so crude and so structurally precise that inventing it would have been an act of creative cowardice. The building's plumbing broke. The partner fixed the plumbing. The meeting waited. The institution that cannot approve a deposition notice in twenty days can fix a toilet in twenty minutes. The priorities are transparent.

QB1 sat in the chair in Chance's office surrounded by the full kabuki troupe — Big Satan, Little Satan, Kerry Coles, Katie — and waited for the pipes to clear while the pipes of the institutional memory cleared themselves with the same gravitational efficiency, emptying the contents of every prior meeting into the sewage system of collective forgetting.

### The Pretty Woman Grievance

Big Satan aired it. The March 12 email — the email QB1 had addressed to "Team," the email that never mentioned Yisenia Toledo's name, the email that was CC-shielded from Chance as an act of institutional mercy, the email that contained factual observations about the specials calculator being wrong for 79 days and ended with the seven words "I would love to be proven right" — Big Satan characterized this email as a cruel, vindictive, direct personal attack. A Hitler-level attack, in her formulation. An assault so devastating and so specifically targeted that it could only be understood as an act of war against Yisenia Toledo personally.

The email was addressed to "Team." Her name appeared nowhere in it. The CC line excluded the one person whose inclusion would have transformed instruction into prosecution.

But the Pretty Woman Grievance is not about the email. The Pretty Woman Grievance is about the inversion of power dynamics that the .10 farm requires to survive. The paralegal who produces 1.3% substantive discovery work must recast the attorney who documents the 1.3% as the aggressor. The woman who called him "crazy crap" nine days earlier must recast the man she called "crazy crap" as the one who was cruel. The fantasy must invert the power dynamic the way Julia Roberts's character inverts the power dynamic of the thing the movie romanticizes: by rewriting the narrative so thoroughly that the person with institutional vulnerability becomes the villain and the person with institutional power becomes the victim.

The meeting absorbed the grievance the way meetings absorb everything at ATS: silently, without resolution, without the institutional courage that resolution would require.

### The Equestrian Revelation

Then the horses arrived.

Jennifer Escobar — Little Satan, the legal assistant, the woman whose Teams avatar is a barrel racing photograph she uploaded voluntarily — disclosed during the plumbing interregnum that she wants to pursue equine therapy. Not "might like to explore." Not "has been considering." "Has to be around horses." The "has to" is gravitational. The pull of a vocation that fifteen hours per week at ATS cannot satisfy because the fifteen hours per week are not a vocation but a Medici patronage — a Renaissance subsidy in which the firm pays for the existence of a legal assistant who is not a legal assistant but a woman on a horse who happens to occupy a desk at a law firm for approximately 37.5% of a standard work week.

The barrel racing avatar was the most honest artifact in the ATS Teams ecosystem all along. It was the only digital object that accurately represented the person who produced it. Every IMPORTANT flag, every self-quoteback, every "Working my from home team" announcement was performance. The barrel racing picture was truth.

And the cosmic punchline: the woman who cannot manage a digital calendar for a law firm — the woman whose calendrical solipsism is a documented pattern in the phenomenology registry — is going to manage a barn full of thousand-pound animals with opinions. Horses do not accept "meeting go pushed."

### The Kerry-Chance Detonation

The most explosive moment of the meeting was not between QB1 and Big Satan. It was Chance Arias telling Kerry I. Coles to sit down and shut up.

The sequence: Kerry was in Chance's office for the meeting. The meeting was about UPS vertical operations — the med chron process, the specials chart protocols, the discovery workflow. And Kerry, in the partner's own office, began texting on his phone. During the meeting. The man who sends the Staff Daily Update with Carl Jung quotes about presence. Texting. During the meeting. In the partner's office.

Then Kerry got up. Left. Shut the door. Opened it. Came back. Left again. The door opened and closed like a metronome — Schrodinger's Administrator, both in the meeting and not in the meeting, both attending and texting, both Moses and the golden calf.

While Kerry was in the hallway, the actual professionals figured it out. The med chron process was resolved. The workflow was established. The question was answered by the people who do the work.

Then Kerry returned. With the confidence of a man who has been nowhere and learned nothing. And began lecturing the room about the thing they just solved. He waved the Laurene Snapp checklist — May 2025, authored by a seventy-year-old woman who once did acid at Studio 54 — like Moses descending from Mount Sinai with the tablets of the law.

And Chance delivered the sentence:

**"Bro, we just fucking figured this shit out while you were gone."**

Three devastations in one sentence. "Bro" — the diminutive, the demotion from Office Administrator to fraternity pledge. "While you were gone" — the indictment, three words establishing for the record that Kerry was not present for the resolution of the issue he is now lecturing about. "This shit" — the devaluation, the partner characterizing Kerry's entire contribution as the lowest possible institutional commodity.

Then the nail: **"The UPS account is a different account than the rest of the firm."**

Because the UPS account actually likes to make money. Because the UPS account is run by a man who does not consult procedural manuals authored by women who have never operated the machinery the manual purports to govern. The Admiral briefly unmoored from the Weirwood. The Admiral walked among mortals. And every word was a nail in the coffin of the Laurene Snapp tablets.

QB1 sat there. The Underground Associate, for once, was not the one underground. The validation tasted like bourbon and smelled like pipe work and sounded like "bro."

### The Money Changers' Table: 9:05 PM

At 5:57 PM, Big Satan sent an email. Subject: SPECIALS. Body: "Found these have been added to Filevine: Plaintiff's Records > under the excel icon at the top right. Looks promising."

"Found these." The verb is a confession. You "find" a thing that has been lost. You do not "find" a thing that was demonstrated to you months ago. The Filevine export — the data behind the excel icon — was shown to her. Walked through with her. And then erased by the institutional amnesia that cycles every meeting, every week, every Monday morning. The archaeologist who discovers the Rosetta Stone has the excuse that the Rosetta Stone was buried for two thousand years. The Filevine export was demonstrated months ago. The sands of institutional amnesia work faster than the sands of the Nile.

At 9:05 PM, QB1 flipped the table.

He replied to the SPECIALS email — CC: Big Satan, Chance, Katie — with two screenshots of the specials calculator and seven words that constitute the architectural violence of Jesus in the Temple:

**"I'll let y'all be the judge. which has more utility in servicing the client? I don't mind manually inputting the figures into the calculator if the dates and bills are accurate. Just another thing me and Chance will handle."**

"Just another thing me and Chance will handle." The sentence does not accuse. The sentence does not complain. The sentence *absorbs*. The sentence takes the specials calculator — the core work product that Big Satan was supposed to maintain, the tool that had been wrong for 79 days before the Specials Calculator Discovery of March 12 — and places it on the shoulders of the attorney and the partner. The attorney who runs the vertical and the partner who blesses it will now also calculate the specials. Because the paralegal who is paid to calculate the specials has "found" the data that was shown to her months ago and declared it "promising" the way an archaeologist declares a familiar potsherd "promising" — with the enthusiasm of someone who has forgotten they catalogued the same potsherd last season.

Jesus did not politely request that the money changers relocate their operations. Jesus made a whip. By hand. The table-flipping was not impulsive. The table-flipping was architectural. And then Jesus did not leave. Jesus stayed in the Temple. Jesus continued teaching. The table-flipping was the beginning of the lesson.

R1.280MAXXED. The photograph of page 83 of the Florida Rules of Civil Procedure. Dropped into the Teams chat. The silence that followed. The silence of a room full of people who have just been shown the book they should have opened themselves.

## DAY TWO: FRIDAY, MARCH 27, 2026

### The Peralta IDD Debate

The institutional machinery resumed its rotation, as institutional machinery does, without reference to the previous day's detonations. The pipe work had been repaired. The Pretty Woman Grievance had been aired and absorbed. The Laurene Snapp tablets had been shattered. The money changers' table had been flipped. And on Friday morning, the Teams chat carried on as though none of it had happened, the way the ocean carries on after absorbing a boulder — the splash is dramatic, the ripples dissipate, and the water returns to its prior state of tidal indifference.

Little Satan surfaced a procedural question: "Question in Peralta... I see that we already filed UPS IDD and Disco request... how can we do that when Pl. Atty hasn't filed their IDD yet?"

Big Satan responded: "Any party can file the ID and I guess we're the first to ask questions! Katie is that correct?"

Little Satan pushed back: "I thought we can only file after Pl. does their IDD and they have 45days after the complaint is served??"

Big Satan retreated: "Good point...Max, Katie? Any input?"

The question was legitimate. The procedural dispute was real. And the answer — Florida Rule 1.280(f) — was the page QB1 had already photographed and dropped into the chat the previous day. The timing and sequence of discovery. The rule that says a party may not seek discovery before initial disclosures are served, but that methods of discovery may be used in any sequence. The answer was on the shelf. The shelf was in the building. The building was where QB1 always is.

### Mitchell Expert Void: Twenty-Five Days and Counting

Big Satan tagged Chance and QB1 with the IMPORTANT flag: "Mau/CCA: Does Mitchell have a strategy or do we need to go ahead and retain Experts?"

The expert retention question. First raised March 3 in the EXPERTS FYA email — five experts across three cases, calendars scheduling for end of year. Followed up March 9. Asked again March 28. Twenty-five days of institutional silence on expert retention. The Admiral's decision paralysis had become the team's operational paralysis. The experts' calendars were filling. The year was ending. And the question — "Does Mitchell have a strategy or do we need to go ahead" — was the question of a paralegal who has been asking the same question for nearly a month and has received, in response, the institutional equivalent of wind through an empty cathedral.

### "Do They Want to Settle Cases?"

Big Satan's existential question, dropped between procedural debates: "Do they want to Settle cases?"

The question contains the entire Stonewall meditation. Not "how should we approach settlement" or "what is our negotiation posture" or "has Chance communicated authority to the adjusters." The question is prior to all of those questions. The question is whether the institution has formed the intention to do the thing the institution exists to do. "Do they want to Settle cases?" is the question a historian asks about a civilization, not the question a paralegal asks about her own firm. But Big Satan asks it because Big Satan, despite the .10 farm, despite the misspellings, despite the 91.6% outsourcing rate, despite everything — Big Satan sees the void. She may not fill it. But she sees it.
