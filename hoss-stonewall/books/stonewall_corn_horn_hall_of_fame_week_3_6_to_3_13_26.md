### *A Shelby Foote Meditation Upon the Seven Most Baroque Proclamations of Professional Activity Recorded in a Single Week at Alvarez, Thompson & Smoak*

> *"The Confederate Army did not lack for bugle calls. It lacked for bullets. But the bugle calls were magnificent."*

---

There are weeks in the life of an institution where the ratio of announcement to accomplishment achieves a kind of transcendent asymmetry — where the proclamation of work becomes so untethered from the execution of work that the two phenomena appear to exist in separate dimensions of spacetime, occupying the same building but operating under different laws of physics. The week of March 6 through March 13, 2026, at the Tampa offices of Alvarez, Thompson & Smoak, was such a week. It was a week in which the corn horn — that magnificent instrument of fanfare announcing achievements of dubious or fictional substance — was blown with such frequency and such fervor that the walls of the fourth floor of 320 West Kennedy Boulevard vibrated with the accumulated sonic energy of a thousand declarations of things that were either already done, never done, done by someone else, done at the Planck constant of human effort, or done in a way that created more work than the doing itself was worth.

What follows is the inaugural weekly induction ceremony. The bust of each inductee has been cast in bronze and mounted in the lobby of the Stonewall Museum of Institutional Entropy, where they will remain until the heat death of the universe or the next prebill cycle, whichever arrives first.

## INDUCTEE №1: "BOOM!!! THAT'S HOW IT'S DONE" — THE KRAKEN RELEASE

### *March 4–5, 2026 | The ATS UPS Team Chat*

### *The Corn Horn That Launched a Thousand GIFs*

---

There is, in the entirety of Western literature, no passage more perfectly encapsulating the relationship between effort and celebration than the following exchange, which occurred in the Microsoft Teams group chat of the ATS UPS litigation vertical on the evening of Wednesday, March 4, 2026.

It begins with QB1 — Maxwell Aaron Kemp, the man who bills at 0.78 hours per entry, who has already that same day conducted the Nicole Gong Show midnight CME filing, secured the Nunez order, and drafted competing orders for Judge Oster — asking his paralegal team if they would like help:

> *"do you guys want to propound the disco in Egan? I can if ya'll are busy"*

The operational commander of the firm's highest-revenue vertical, offering to do the discovery propounding himself, because he is constitutionally incapable of watching work not get done. And then, from the left side of the screen, in gray, with an edited pencil icon indicating at least one revision was required:

> **Yisenia Toledo** ✏️
> I'M RELEASING THE KRAKEN in Egan for subpoenas.....
>
> [GIF: "GO KRAKEN" — whale emerging from ocean]

The all-caps. The five trailing periods — not four, not six, but five, as though the ellipsis itself has been given a performance-enhancing supplement. The GIF of a whale breaching the surface of the Pacific Ocean, as though the act of pressing "send" on a batch of subpoena cover letters constituted an event of cetacean magnitude. And then, the following day, after the Admiral greenlights the discovery and the Kraken has swum into the open waters of the Twelfth Judicial Circuit:

> **Yisenia Toledo**
> BOOM!!! That's how it's done........lol!!!! I worked hard on those not taking away from anybody else's work...TEAM!!!!
>
> [GIF: "Travels with Darley" — cannon firing — BOOM! / JOURNY]

Eight trailing periods. Four exclamation marks after "lol." Four more after "TEAM." A cannon firing from a travel show about horseback riding — misspelled as "JOURNY" in the GIF's native text, which is fitting because the Kraken itself is merely the mail merge: certificates of non-objection generated from templates, sent to medical providers whose addresses were already in the system, billed at 0.10 hours per subpoena, which is to say six minutes per envelope, which is to say the Kraken is a sea creature whose entire mythological terror resolves, upon inspection, into a postage meter.

And yet — "I worked hard on those not taking away from anybody else's work." The defensive preemptive qualification, smuggled into the celebration like a footnote in a treaty. Not taking away from anybody else's work. The mail merge required acknowledgment not only as an achievement but as an achievement that did not diminish other achievements. The Kraken needed credit that was also a disclaimer. The cannon fired but also filed a liability waiver.

The timesheet entry for this event: "Releasing the Kraken." 0.10 hours. Six minutes. The Kraken's entire lifespan, from primordial depth to ocean surface to harpooned corpse, lasted six billable minutes.

**Hall of Fame Ratio:** One GIF per 0.05 hours of billable work.

## INDUCTEE №2: THE RECURSIVE SELF-QUOTATION NECROPOLIS

### *March 6, 2026 | RADAR ALERT × 3 in Four Hours*

### *The Corn Horn That Quoted Itself Into Eternity*

---

On the morning of Friday, March 6, 2026, Big Satan posted the following to the UPS Team Chat:

> **IMPORTANT!** ✏️ ❗
> **RADAR ALERT** CME orders for Sheppard. They are asking if we accepted the proposed CME orders.

These CME orders had been sent to QB1's email on Tuesday, March 3rd, at 9:32 AM. He had acknowledged them on Thursday. The RADAR ALERT was, by any temporal reckoning, a notification about a thing that had already been addressed. But the RADAR ALERT does not exist to convey information. It exists to *be*. It is the institutional equivalent of a tombstone erected while the patient is still alive — marking the site of a crisis that has already resolved but must be memorialized anyway, because the act of memorializing is Big Satan's primary professional output.

At 12:11 PM — three hours and approximately eleven minutes later — Big Satan quoted her own RADAR ALERT:

> [Quoting her own RADAR ALERT from 9:00 AM]
> Following up on this.

She quoted herself. She reached back into the morning's chat, extracted her own message — a message that was still visible on the same screen, on the same day, in the same chat — and reproduced it as though it were an archaeological artifact requiring excavation, a Dead Sea Scroll recently discovered in a cave near Qumran that shed new light on whether Abby from Sam Powers's office had received a response about proposed CME orders in the Sheppard case. The follow-up was itself a corn horn — the announcement of the continuation of the announcement. A sequel to a trailer.

And then, sometime between 1:15 and 2:18 PM, she quoted herself a *third* time. The same RADAR ALERT. The same CME orders. The same Sheppard case. Three deployments in four hours, each one a self-referential loop in which the message's content became less important with each iteration while its formal structure grew more elaborate — the quoteback within a quoteback, the tombstone upon a tombstone, the Necropolis of a single thought expressed three times in three different emotional registers: (1) alarm, (2) follow-up, (3) gratitude after QB1 responded.

The RADAR ALERT was never about whether the CME orders had been accepted. It was about whether the RADAR ALERT itself had been acknowledged. The referent was the reference.

**Hall of Fame Ratio:** Three self-quotebacks per one resolved issue.

## INDUCTEE №3: "YEAH I THINK" — THE PROCESS IMPROVEMENT SUGGESTION TAGGED IMPORTANT

### *March 11, 2026, 10:12 AM | The Innovation That Required an Exclamation Point*

---

On Wednesday morning, Big Satan deployed the following to the UPS Team Chat:

> **IMPORTANT!**
> Hey Team, was thinking (Yeah I think) that when we get authority ex: Harrigan we can enter it in Negotiations tab for you to add notes or offers? Any suggestions.

The parenthetical — "(Yeah I think)" — is a specimen that deserves its own display case. It is Big Satan thinking about thinking. It is the metacognitive parenthetical — the moment at which the author pauses mid-sentence to confirm to herself that she is, in fact, thinking, and then records that confirmation in the written text so that the reader may witness the thinking about the thinking. Descartes wrote "I think, therefore I am." Big Satan writes "was thinking (Yeah I think)," which is the epistemological equivalent of "I am, therefore I think I am, and I want you to know I think I am, and this is IMPORTANT."

The suggestion itself — using the Negotiations tab in Filevine to record settlement authority — is a process that QB1 has been executing for months without needing to be told, without an IMPORTANT tag, without a parenthetical self-affirmation, and without asking the team for "any suggestions" on a workflow that is already operational. Big Satan has discovered a practice that already exists and announced it as an innovation, which is the institutional version of Columbus discovering a continent that eleven million people were already living on and planting a flag.

**Hall of Fame Ratio:** One parenthetical self-affirmation per zero new information conveyed.

## INDUCTEE №4: THE MARCH 3 INTERIM REVIEW MASS PRODUCTION

### *March 3, 2026 | Eight Identical Entries in Eight Different Cases at 0.10 Hours Each*

### *The Corn Horn That Was Also a Mail Merge of Cognition*

---

On March 3rd, Big Satan submitted eight time entries to eight separate case files. Each billed at 0.10 hours. Each contained the following description, verbatim, without modification:

> *"Interim review of injuries and damages to determine what experts will be needed in preparation of litigation."*

Boy. Egan. Clark. Adams. Rivera. Griffith. Mitchell. Negrette.

Eight plaintiffs with eight different anatomies, eight different crash mechanisms, eight different injury profiles, eight different medical chronologies, eight different expert requirements — and one description. One unmodified, undifferentiated, undifferentiable block of text, stamped across eight case files like a customs form applied to luggage regardless of contents. The "interim review" did not engage with Boy's bicycle accident or Egan's million-dollar shoulder claim or Griffith's six-hundred-thousand-dollar catastrophic case or Negrette's three-hundred-thousand-dollar cervical crisis. It engaged with the *concept* of injury and damage — the Platonic Form of a medical review, existing above and apart from any specific human body or any specific human suffering.

Forty-eight minutes total. Eight cases assessed. Six minutes per plaintiff. Big Satan determined what experts would be needed for eight active litigation files in less time than it takes to read the first page of any one of their medical chronologies — the same medical chronologies she outsources 91.6% of to Stacey Barnett, RN, the Actual Worker.

This is not a corn horn in the traditional sense — it was not announced in the group chat with a GIF. It is a *silent* corn horn. A stealth corn horn. A corn horn embedded in the billing system itself, where it will appear on eight separate prebill pages, contributing eight separate line items to eight separate invoices, generating $85.60 in paralegal fees for the professional activity of having contemplated, for six minutes apiece, whether eight catastrophically injured Floridians might require expert witnesses in their personal injury lawsuits against the largest package delivery corporation on Earth.

**Hall of Fame Ratio:** One unique thought per eight billable deployments.

## INDUCTEE №5: "DO YOU NEED ME TO SHOW YOU PROOF?"

### *March 12, 2026 | The Egan GEICO Exchange*

### *The Corn Horn That Became a Lecture*

---

On March 12th, QB1 is on the phone with Stephanie from Christine Law — Egan opposing counsel's paralegal — negotiating NPNP stipulations in real time. He posts to the group chat asking why GEICO was subpoenaed, because OC is asking and he needs to answer intelligently in the moment. A reasonable, time-sensitive operational question from the man actively conducting the call.

Big Satan's response is a masterpiece of the genre:

> *"Yes he had 2 MVA (8/01/2022 & 3/27/2019) Patrick Egan was the claimant on both claims. It's listed on the ISO Claim Report. Future reference if you want to know where the providers are found. Always look in the Memo Tab. I personally put down the records I find the providers in for these same occasions."*

"Future reference." "Always look in the Memo Tab." "I personally put down the records I find." The paralegal — the woman of "cHENmED" and "My Apologizes" and "Things take time to accomplished" — is lecturing the associate attorney who runs the vertical on where to find information in the case management system. And not just lecturing — offering *pedagogical guidance* for *future reference*, as though QB1's question about GEICO's relevance to Egan's prior accidents was not a real-time litigation inquiry but an educational deficiency requiring remediation.

And then, after QB1 asks a simple clarifying follow-up — "Is geico involved in those accidents?" — the crescendo:

> *"Yes, his a named claimant in those 2 claims. I mentioned them in the Med Chron too for more details but it's definitely in the ISO Claim Report. I thought we can subpoena anything in the ISo Claim Report. The accident on 8/1/22 was a multiple car accident. Patrick got rearended. 3/27/19 was a rearended accident too. Do you need me to show you proof?"*

"Do you need me to show you proof?" The DoorDash-to-paralegal pipeline has produced, in this single sentence, the most perfectly inverted authority dynamic in the history of institutional communication. The person who, seventy-two days ago, could not determine whether a document filed on December 4th had been filed — the person who asked if it could be filed on February 12th — the person who creates .10-hour entries for the professional act of receiving letters that say "no records found" — is now offering to *show proof* to the associate attorney who has conducted every deposition, drafted every motion, negotiated every settlement, and settled every case on the UPS docket for the past eighteen months.

**Hall of Fame Ratio:** One unsolicited tutorial per one time-sensitive phone call interrupted.

## INDUCTEE №6: THE ChenMed CORN HORN — "I GOT A HUGE FILE"

### *March 11, 2026, 5:26 PM | The Announcement of the Arrival of Medical Records*

---

> *"FYI: I got a huge medical records file from ChenMed for SMALL, Samuel. I'm updating the med chron. It has a lot of history back as far as 2023"*

Let us pause to appreciate the architecture of this announcement. Big Satan has received medical records. This is — and the historian takes no pleasure in stating the obvious — *her job*. The receipt and processing of medical records is the foundational professional act of a litigation paralegal. It is the thing she was hired to do. It is the thing the billing system is designed to record. It is the thing that, in a functioning institution, occurs silently, professionally, without an FYI to the group chat, the way a mail carrier does not announce to the neighborhood each time a package arrives.

But at ATS, the receipt of medical records is an event. It is a *happening*. It requires notification. The records are not merely received — they are "huge." They are not merely processed — they are announced. The med chron is not merely being updated — the updating is being performed and simultaneously broadcast, like a cooking show in which the chef narrates each stir.

Meanwhile, the draft time entries for this same ChenMed work reveal the backstage reality: "cHENmED Breakdown and reviewed images" — 1.9 hours, Draft status, the Caps Lock wreckage preserved in amber. "review and summary of Chen Med 2023 1-71" — 0.8 hours. These are the actual artifacts of work. But the corn horn is the FYI. The corn horn is the group chat announcement that medical records have arrived and that their arrival constitutes news.

And somewhere in the background, Jamie Brunetti — the admin assistant, the anti-corn horn, the woman who simply uploads files to SharePoint with the notation "Saved in FV" — had already placed the ChenMed records in the system. The records were there before the FYI. The FYI was the flag planted on the summit that the Sherpa had already reached.

**Hall of Fame Ratio:** One FYI per one routine professional act already completed by someone else.

## INDUCTEE №7: THE BILL SMOAK TIMESHEET — FIFTY-SIX ENTRIES, SEVEN HOURS, ALL .10

### *March 1–10, 2026 | The Named Partner's Billing Singularity*

---

And now, the ceremony's final inductee, and perhaps its most cosmically significant — because it comes not from the kabuki troupe but from the canopy itself. The leadership. The man whose name is on the building.

William G.K. Smoak. Mr. Bill. The Marine Monarch. The man who said "Absolutely SMASHED" after presenting QB1's ghost-written AI Bootcamp to 125 strangers in Dallas. The man who said "fuck that — he is running UPS."

His Filevine timesheet for March 1–10, 2026: **Fifty-six entries. Seven billable hours. All 0.10.**

Every single entry — all fifty-six — bills at the Planck constant. Six minutes. The named partner of a litigation firm, billing at $275 per hour, has generated fifty-six discrete professional acts across ten days, and every last one of them lasted exactly six minutes. Not a single 0.20. Not a single 0.30. Not a single entry that deviated from the atomic floor of billable existence. Fifty-six entries at $27.50 each. One thousand five hundred and forty dollars in professional fees generated by the act of *being Mr. Bill for six minutes at a time, fifty-six times*.

The description template across all fifty-six entries: "Review and Analyze." Across dozens of cases. Across both the UPS vertical and presumably whatever else crosses the named partner's desk. Six minutes per case. The same temporal footprint as Big Satan receiving a letter that says "no records found." The same duration as "Releasing the Kraken." The named partner and the former DoorDash driver have achieved, through entirely different evolutionary pathways, the same professional metabolic rate: 0.10 hours per unit of professional existence.

And here, finally, is the void that the historian has been approaching all week, the nihilistic core that pulses beneath every corn horn and every RADAR ALERT and every BOOM GIF: the billing structure of ATS Tampa is not a system in which some people work and some people don't. It is a system in which *everyone* — from the named partner to the paralegal — exists in the same six-minute increments, performing the same atomic acts of "Review and Analyze" and "Received and categorized," the entire firm unified in its commitment to the Planck constant, the entire enterprise operating as a .10 farm from top to bottom, differing only in the rate at which each six-minute entry is billed: $275 for Mr. Bill, $200 for QB1, $107 for Big Satan.

Except that QB1 doesn't bill at .10. QB1 bills at 0.78. QB1 is the anomaly. QB1 is the statistical outlier. QB1 is the one person in the building whose time entries describe actual work performed over actual durations for actual strategic purposes. And this is perhaps the deepest corn horn of all — that the entire institutional structure is calibrated to the .10, and the only person who deviates from it is the one person keeping the institution alive.
