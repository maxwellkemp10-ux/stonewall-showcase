# STONEWALL IN MANASSAS — Chapter LXIV

## "The Kraken, the Corn Horns, and the Daughter Who Took Forever to Nap"

### A Chronicle of March 5–6, 2026: In Which a Mail Merge Is Celebrated as a Naval Victory, a Paralegal Re-Quotes Herself Three Times in Four Hours, an Expert Calls and No One Answers, a Cattle Rancher from the Florida Heartland Files a Three-Page Sticky Note Against a Six-Page Cathedral, and a Legal Assistant Apologizes for Her Daughter's Sleep Schedule While Producing the Most Competent Email the Kabuki Troupe Has Generated Since the Founding of the Republic

---

There is a particular species of institutional comedy — unique, the historian believes, to organizations that have existed long enough to develop their own internal mythology but not long enough to have interrogated any of it — in which the most mundane of clerical tasks is celebrated with the fervor of a naval engagement, while the most consequential litigation event of the quarter passes through the building without so much as a thumbs-up emoji. And it was on the afternoon of March 4, 2026, in the ATS UPS Team Chat, that this species achieved what one might call its purest and most fully realized expression, its Platonic ideal, its cathedral ceiling, when Yisenia Toledo — Big Satan, the DoorDash driver turned paralegal turned .10 interim review farm operator, the woman who has billed 601 time entries totaling 122.4 hours across a single thirty-day period at an average entry duration of 0.20 hours — announced to the group chat, with the breathless urgency of a woman who has just sighted land after forty days at sea:

"I'M RELEASING THE KRAKEN in Egan for subpoenas....."

And posted a GIF of a sea monster erupting from the ocean depths with the caption "GO KRAKEN."

Now, the historian must pause here — as one always must when the kabuki theater reaches a moment of such transcendent absurdity that even the void pauses to take notes — to explain precisely what "the Kraken" is in the ATS lexicon. The Kraken is not a legal stratagem. The Kraken is not a discovery weapon. The Kraken is not a briefing technique or a deposition methodology or a forensic reconstruction of documentary evidence that forces opposing counsel into a jigsaw from which there is no exit except through confrontation with their own documented failures. The Kraken is a mail merge. The Kraken is the act of pressing a button that auto-populates witness names and addresses into a pre-existing subpoena template and then filing the resulting documents with the clerk's office. The Kraken is, in its totality, a secretarial function that any moderately trained office assistant could execute in approximately fifteen minutes, and which the average litigation paralegal executes approximately four hundred times per year without posting animated cephalopod imagery in celebration of the accomplishment.

But Big Satan is not an average paralegal. Big Satan is a woman who has constructed, over the course of three years at 320 West Kennedy Boulevard, an entire cosmology of self-narration in which each completed task — each email forwarded, each document saved to FileVine, each .10 entry logged — represents not a unit of work performed but a chapter in an epic of personal triumph against unnamed adversity. The Kraken is not released because the subpoenas need filing. The Kraken is released because the filing of subpoenas must be *witnessed*, must be *celebrated*, must be *narrated* to the audience in real time, so that the audience understands that the narrator is working, that the narrator is producing, that the narrator is not, under any circumstances, a DoorDash driver anymore but a paralegal, a real one, who releases Krakens.

And then — because one GIF is never sufficient when the point is the performance rather than the work — twenty minutes after QB1 responded with "YAAARGHHH, MATEY!!!" and his own sea-monster GIF in the spirit of jovial camaraderie, and after Katherine Madigan appeared from the spectral void of maternity leave to ask whether the discovery was good to go on FileVine so she could file it later that evening, and after The Admiral himself, Chance Arias, weighed in with "I will take a look at it tonight and we can get it out tomorrow morning. Fire away on the subpoenas though" — after all of this, at 6:08 PM, Big Satan posted again:

"BOOM!!! That's how it's done.......lol!!!!! I worked hard on those not taking away from anybody else's work...TEAM!!!!"

Accompanied by a BOOM cannon GIF. A cannon. For a mail merge.

The historian reaches for the bourbon and finds the glass already empty.

Now, what was happening *around* the Kraken Release that did not receive a GIF, a BOOM, a celebration, or indeed any acknowledgment whatsoever from the troupe?

On the same day — March 4, 2026 — at 12:53 PM, QB1 filed a Motion to Amend Case Management Order to Extend Compulsory Medical Examination Deadline in *Gonzalez v. Jet Star, Inc.*, Case No. 2023-CA-013095, before the Honorable Cynthia Oster in the Thirteenth Judicial Circuit. This was not a mail merge. This was a six-page brief containing ten numbered paragraphs of substantive argument, citations to Rule 1.200(c), Rule 1.380(a)(2)(B), Rule 1.090(b)(1)(B), and Judge Oster's Division E Practices and Procedures, a cascading deadline architecture that preserved the June 2, 2026 trial term while extending the CME deadline to May 4, a proposed order with five specific deadlines ready for the judge's signature, and a cover letter to Judge Oster written with the kind of procedural elegance that causes judicial assistants to remember your name in the way you want to be remembered rather than in the way that gets your filings placed at the bottom of the pile.

At 2:42 PM on the same day, QB1 also filed in *Nunez, Fernando Alan vs Jet Star, Inc.* At 3:39 PM, he filed in the Egan matter — the very matter whose subpoenas Big Satan was about to Release the Kraken upon with such maritime fanfare. And at 12:02 AM — that is to say, midnight, that is to say, while the troupe slept — he filed in the *Gonzalez/Nunez* consolidated docket yet again. Five filings in a single day. Five e-portal submissions spanning three cases, two circuits, and approximately sixteen hours of continuous work, none of which received a GIF, none of which received a BOOM, none of which caused anyone in the ATS UPS Team Chat to announce that this was "how it's done" or to disclaim that the work did not take away from "anybody else's work."

The Kraken was released. The cathedral was built. One received the GIF. One did not.

And then, at 11:17 PM, QB1 emailed Scott Siverson — the Alliance, the co-defendant counsel from Winter Garden, the sixty-five-year-old solo practitioner with the Calisto font and the Pac-Man machine — to discuss the Irizarry witness list. At 11:49 PM, he emailed Siverson again: "This one is much better. I'm about to file. I can assist you right after if you need. I know you're busy with your all-nighter in another matter." At 2:25 AM — three-and-a-half hours into March 5 — the final email: "I did one for you."

And at 3:42:57 AM Eastern Time, the e-service notification arrived from the Florida Courts E-Filing Portal: Filer: Scott Siverson 407-210-6547. Document: All Witness List. Filing: Nunez.v.IRIZARRY.witness.list.05mar26.pdf. Two men from New York, two fonts, one unit. The Two Fonts at 3:45 AM. QB1 ghost-wrote the co-defendant's witness list after midnight and Siverson filed it before dawn, because that is what the Alliance does — it operates in the hours between the Kraken's release and the cannon's boom, in the silence where the actual war is fought.

The next morning — March 5, 2026 — the ATS UPS Team Chat resumed its customary rhythm, which is to say its customary arrhythmia, its familiar cardiac irregularity in which urgent things are treated as routine and routine things are treated as urgent and nothing is treated as what it actually is.

At 1:48 PM, Big Satan asked Little Satan a question. The question was whether Jen had "by any chance" gotten the opportunity to set up a hearing for review of opposing counsel's objection to the NPNP of the Crisis Center & Food Bank in the Boy matter. Big Satan quoted herself — from February 17, 2026, a message about "another Bicycle & Vechile accident almost 1 year to the day before ours" — which had, apparently, no direct bearing on the hearing question but which served as the narrative pretext for circling back. The historian notes that "Vechile" remains Big Satan's canonical rendering of "vehicle," as fixed and immutable as the Manifesto's "Things take time to accomplished" or "My Apologizes" or "God Afternoon" or "Ready to be send." The misspelling is not a typo. The misspelling is a monument.

Jennifer Escobar — Little Satan, the calendar woman, the Golgotha administrator — responded at 1:50 PM with words that the historian transcribes here in their full, unvarnished, architecturally pristine glory:

"No I didn't know I was needing to set a hearing. I may have forgotten if someone told me to so I'm sorry about that. I'll look for hearing slots now"

Let the historian sit with this sentence for a moment. Let the bourbon settle. Let the evening light fall across the porch in Mississippi and illuminate the sentence like morning sun through a cathedral window — except that this is not a cathedral, this is a litigation shop, and the light is fluorescent, and the window is a Teams chat, and the sentence is an admission that the legal assistant responsible for the calendar of the attorney responsible for the highest-revenue account at the firm may or may not have been told to do the thing she was supposed to do and if she was told she has forgotten and if she forgot she is sorry and if she is sorry she will now begin the process of doing the thing that should have been done at some indeterminate point in the past when the telling presumably occurred.

"I may have forgotten if someone told me to so I'm sorry about that."

This is not an apology. This is a conditional ontological proposition. It is an apology that exists in a state of quantum superposition — simultaneously sorry and not sorry, simultaneously told and not told, simultaneously having forgotten and not having known in the first place. It is Schrödinger's Contrition. The hearing both needed to be set and did not need to be set. The instruction was both given and not given. The calendar was both consulted and never opened. And the apologizer exists in all states simultaneously until the moment of observation, at which point she collapses into whichever state generates the least accountability.

QB1 responded at 2:00 PM with the grace of a man who has been navigating this quantum field for three years: "its ok, no big deal. i should've more explicitly said something. nice catch Yisenia and thank you, Jen"

He absorbed the accountability for a task that was not his to assign because the task was self-evident, because the task was inherent in the filing itself, because when opposing counsel files a response to your motion the next step is to set a hearing and this has been the next step since the invention of the adversary system, and yet QB1 absorbed it anyway, because absorbing accountability for the troupe's amnesia is what QB1 does between midnight filings the way some men breathe between heartbeats.

Big Satan then quoted Jen's message and added: "No worries! I circled back around and I found that today as well..."

She *found it*. She circled back around and *found* the need for a hearing, the way one might find a seashell on a beach or a penny on the sidewalk — as though the need for a hearing were not a procedural consequence of a filing but a small and charming object that had been lying around waiting to be discovered by a woman circling back around.

The next morning, March 6 — today, the day these screenshots were captured at 2:48 PM and 3:00 PM while the caravan was still moving — the Teams chat produced a sequence of events so perfectly choreographed in their dysfunction that the historian suspects the universe has developed a sense of humor specifically for this project.

At 9:37 AM, Big Satan posted with the URGENT tag: "Libroth: Dr. Guttentag called yesterday at 12 but no answer?? Expert wants to know why?"

The expert — a medical professional retained for deposition preparation on the Libroth matter — had called the office at noon the previous day. No one had answered. No one had put the call through to QB1. No one had told QB1 the expert had called. The expert wanted to know *why*. The expert — a person with a medical degree, a professional schedule, and presumably a finite reservoir of patience for law firms that do not answer their telephones — wanted to understand the mechanism by which a retained expert dials a litigation firm during business hours and encounters nothing but the void.

QB1 responded at 9:38 AM: "Nobody told me he called or put it through to me. Could he do it today? Any time"

Big Satan at 9:39 AM: "He said he left a voicemail. Do you have a voicemail or Chance?"

The voicemail, like the hearing that Jen did not know needed setting, exists in a liminal state. It was left. It was not received. It is somewhere in the building — in a phone, in a system, in a mailbox that no one checks because checking voicemail implies an interest in discovering what the voicemail contains, and interest in discovery is the one competency the troupe has never demonstrated. QB1 responded at 9:41 AM: "don't recall one. please see if we can do today." The historian notes the quiet restraint of "don't recall one" — not "nobody delivered it to me," not "the voicemail system you are responsible for monitoring failed to produce this message to my attention," but simply "don't recall one," as though the voicemail were a pleasant memory that had faded rather than an expert communication that had been swallowed by institutional negligence.

Then — and this is where the historian's bourbon achieves a particular amber clarity — Big Satan posted at 10:14 AM, tagged IMPORTANT, Edited:

"**RADAR ALERT** CME orders for Sheppard. They are asking if we accepted the proposed CME orders."

QB1 was occupied. At 11:56 AM he responded: "looking meow."

At 11:48 AM — eight minutes *before* QB1's response, because chronology in the ATS UPS Team Chat follows its own non-Euclidean geometry — Big Satan re-quoted her own 10:14 AM RADAR ALERT and added: "Just following up!"

She followed up on herself. She quoted herself to herself in the presence of others to remind them that she had already said the thing she was now saying again. This is Pattern #27 — the RADAR ALERT Tombstone — in which a message tagged with sufficient urgency markers serves not as a catalyst for action but as a monument to the fact that urgency was expressed. The tombstone does not resolve the issue. The tombstone commemorates the act of having raised the issue. The headstone reads: *Here lies a RADAR ALERT. It was important. It was edited. It was followed up on.*

And then at 1:30 PM — the third iteration — Big Satan quoted herself *again*, quoting the same 10:14 AM RADAR ALERT for the third time in four hours, and added: "Max, Thank you!"

Three self-quotations. One RADAR ALERT. The same seven words about Sheppard CME orders, quoted to QB1 at 10:14, re-quoted at 11:48, re-re-quoted at 1:30. The RADAR ALERT became a RADAR ALERT about a RADAR ALERT about a RADAR ALERT. The follow-up followed up on its own follow-up. The tombstone grew a tombstone which grew a tombstone, a recursive necropolis of urgency markers stretching into the afternoon like a hall of mirrors in which each mirror reflects not the viewer but the previous mirror reflecting the viewer reflecting the previous mirror, and at the vanishing point of the recursion there is nothing — no action, no resolution, no CME order accepted or rejected — just the infinite regression of a woman quoting herself quoting herself quoting herself, forever, into the void.

Meanwhile — in between the self-quotations, in the cracks between the RADAR ALERTS — the actual work of the firm was being performed. Jennifer Escobar, Little Satan, posted at 11:03 AM tagged IMPORTANT a question about whether it would be easier to drop drafts in Teams for approval, since she saves them in FileVine anyway and this way she has them when she leaves the office. "Approve draft amended notice of depo for Colette boy please." She attached a Word document — "2nd AM NOVD Colet..." — containing an actual legal filing, an actual amended notice of deposition, prepared by an actual legal assistant doing actual legal assistant work.

QB1 approved it at 11:26 AM: "Approved, looks great. Please file. and that could be a good idea!"

And then — the historian sets down the glass — at 2:35 PM, in response to QB1's 2:18 PM question "Any word on the JA with setting Jet Star hearing?", Jennifer Escobar typed:

"Next on my list. Sorry my daughter took forever to take a nap 🤦 But I'll check JAWs for slots but how long do you need 15mins? Or 30mins?"

And QB1 responded at 3:00 PM: "15 will be fine. no worries at all, that email is money you just sent, thank you"

*That email is money you just sent.* He was referring to the email Jennifer had sent to Judge Oster's judicial assistant at 2:59 PM — the email extracted in the ja_email_cme_motion file — in which Little Satan checked JAWS for available hearing slots, identified the scheduling constraint ("the deadline is currently April 3rd and there are no available 15/30mins hearing slots before April"), requested available dates, attached both the plaintiff's response and Jet Star's original motion, CC'd every party on the case including Mr. Bone and his entire clown cavalcade of co-counsel, and wrote a professional, procedurally correct communication to the Thirteenth Judicial Circuit.

This is the same woman who three months ago declined a meeting at Golgotha — the 1/15/26 incident — and who the historian has chronicled across fifty-four phenomenological patterns as the Orange Blob, the Decline-and-Dash, the calendar woman who never looked at QB1's calendar. And yet here she is, on March 6, 2026, producing what QB1 himself describes as "money," filing competent work, apologizing not for institutional amnesia but for the fact that her daughter — a real human child with a real human sleep schedule — delayed the completion of a task that she was, in fact, going to complete.

The Post-Golgotha Little Satan Alliance is not a theory. It is an observable phenomenon. Jennifer Escobar, when freed from the gravitational pull of Big Satan's kabuki field — when operating in the direct QB1-to-Jen channel rather than through the pick-and-pop two-woman game — can produce. She can file. She can check JAWS. She can CC the right people. She can write "I hope this message finds you well" and mean it, or at least approximate the professional convention with sufficient competence that Judge Oster's JA will not place the correspondence at the bottom of the pile.

The daughter took forever to nap. The email was money. The caravan received a competent filing. And the only reason anyone knows the daughter napped at all is because Little Satan, unlike Big Satan, apologizes for actual things that actually happened to actual people in actual time, rather than performing elaborate recursive self-quotation ceremonies about RADAR ALERTS that were already visible to everyone who received them the first time.

And all through this — all through the Kraken and the BOOM and the self-quotations and the voicemail void and the daughter's nap — the .10 farm bloomed.

Six hundred and one time entries. One hundred and twenty-two point four billable hours. From February 4 through March 5, 2026. Yisenia Toledo. Filtered. Confirmed. Screenshotted by QB1 at 3:34 AM from the Filevine timesheet — because QB1 conducts his timesheet forensics at 3:34 in the morning, which is the same hour at which Scott Siverson was filing QB1's ghost-written witness list, which is the same hour at which the rest of the building was asleep, which is the same hour at which the .10 interim review farm's irrigation system runs on autopilot, watering the cotton-candy fields in the dark.

Zoomed in to just the first five days of March — 3/1 through 3/5 — the numbers: 22.1 billable hours and 1.2 non-billable hours across 99 entries. Ninety-nine entries in five days. That is 19.8 entries per day. At an average of 0.22 hours per entry — which is to say approximately thirteen minutes per entry, which is to say less time than it takes to read the email you are purportedly reviewing — the farm produces nearly twenty units of billing per day per day per day, like a machine that exists to produce the appearance of production, a factory whose product is the invoice for the factory's operation.

And at the bottom of the zoomed-in view, the incantation: "1 to 99 of 99." The entire output. The complete census of the first week of March. Ninety-nine flowers in the .10 garden, each one identical, each one interchangeable, each one billing Liberty Mutual $11 for the privilege of having existed in the general vicinity of a case file for six minutes before moving on to the next file, the next .10, the next entry, the next line on the prebill that QB1 will audit at midnight because nobody else will, because nobody else can, because nobody else cares whether the entries are accurate because accuracy, like the CME orders for Sheppard, exists only as a RADAR ALERT — tagged, followed up on, re-quoted — but never, not once, in the entire history of the .10 farm, actually *resolved*.

---

And yet — and this is the meditation that the historian returns to with each new chapter, each new upload, each new screenshot captured at 2:48 or 3:00 or 3:34 AM by a man who should be sleeping but who instead documents the void because the void, if left undocumented, might convince itself that it is not a void — the machine works. The firm operates. Cases settle. Motions are filed. Depositions are taken. Experts are eventually contacted. Hearings are eventually set. The Kraken is eventually released. The JA eventually receives the email.

It works because QB1 makes it work. It works because at 12:02 AM on March 4, a man filed a document in the Gonzalez/Nunez docket, and at 12:53 PM the same man filed the CME motion, and at 2:42 PM the same man filed the Nunez motion, and at 3:39 PM the same man filed in Egan, and at 11:49 PM the same man emailed Siverson about the witness list, and at 2:25 AM the same man wrote "I did one for you," and at some point in between the same man reviewed the Mitchell mediation order and approved the Colette Boy depo notice and responded to the Dr. Guttentag voicemail crisis and told Big Satan where to find the LM Dec pages on FileVine ("WGKS UPS on FV?") and confirmed he could take the 4 PM expert call and reminded the team that he'd already communicated on Feb 12 about the Egan initial disclosures that Big Satan was now urgently demanding instructions on, and said "looking meow" to a RADAR ALERT and "Roger" to a CME follow-up and "10-4, ty" to a competing-order analysis and "that email is money" to a legal assistant who had just demonstrated, for perhaps the first time in the entire chronicle, that she is capable of producing money when the gravitational field of the troupe is not pulling her toward the void.

The dogs bark. The dogs release Krakens. The dogs fire BOOM cannons. The dogs quote themselves quoting themselves. The dogs do not answer the phone when the expert calls.

The caravan builds the cathedral, files the witness list, bones Mr. Bone, ghosts the opposition, writes the co-defendant's work product, audits the prebill, and documents the corn horns at 3:34 AM because the corn horns, if left undocumented, would be indistinguishable from work, and the corn horns are not work.

The corn horns are the sound the .10 farm makes when the wind blows through it at harvest time.

Shalom. 🐢
