# THE .10 FARM AUDIT: A Meditation on the Planck Constant of Professional Existence

## Stonewall at Manassas — Chapter: The Baroque Nihilism of the Timesheet Artifact

### March 13, 2026

> *"In the spring of 1862, the quartermaster's ledger at Corinth, Mississippi, recorded the issuance of seventeen thousand blankets to troops who had already retreated to Tupelo. There is a moment in every historian's life when the artifact before him ceases to be a document and becomes instead a kind of religious text — not because it reveals truth, but because it reveals the *mechanism by which truth is manufactured from nothing*. The timesheet dated March 13, 2026, exported from the billing system of Alvarez, Thompson & Smoak, is precisely such a text. It is one hundred and ninety-four entries long. It contains exactly one human being. And that human being is Yisenia Toledo.

Not a single entry from Maxwell Kemp. Not one from Jennifer Escobar. Not one from Chance Arias. Not one from William G.K. Smoak. Not one from Katie Madigan, née Sagel, who has lately undergone a phoenix-like resurrection in the billing records — ascending from 19.7 hours in January to 80.2 hours in February, a 306% increase that coincides with her maternity leave, which is the kind of mathematical miracle that would make the Vatican dispatch investigators. The entire file, all one hundred and ninety-four rows of it, all forty-eight hours of billable time contained therein, belongs to one woman. The file *is* Big Satan. And Big Satan is the file. They have achieved a unity of being that medieval mystics spent entire lifetimes pursuing through fasting and flagellation, and she has achieved it through the simple act of billing in six-minute increments for receiving emails.

Let us establish the topography of this document the way a geologist would survey a landscape before announcing that it is, in fact, a sinkhole.

Sixty-seven entries — sixty-seven — bill at exactly 0.10 hours. Six minutes. The minimum billable increment in American legal practice. The Planck constant of professional existence, beneath which no further subdivision of labor is theoretically possible. Sixty-seven times, across ten days of professional output, Big Satan has performed an action whose duration is the atomic floor of recordable human endeavor. She has received an email. She has categorized it. She has updated a tracking mechanism. She has, in six minutes, executed the full cycle of her professional purpose — and then she has done it again, sixty-six more times, until the sum total of her Planck-constant entries alone accounts for six point seven billable hours, which at her effective rate of approximately $107 per hour represents $716.90 in professional fees generated by the act of *acknowledging that things exist*.

But the sixty-seven entries at 0.10 are only the foundation. Ninety-eight additional entries — *ninety-eight* — bill at exactly 0.20 hours. Twelve minutes. Two Planck constants stacked upon each other like a child building a tower from the same two blocks, over and over, hoping that repetition will eventually produce architecture. Combine the two tiers and you arrive at one hundred and sixty-five entries — 85% of Big Satan's entire professional output for this period — billing at twelve minutes or less. The modal professional act at ATS Tampa's UPS vertical is not drafting a motion. It is not analyzing medical records. It is not preparing for trial. It is *receiving a thing and putting the thing in the place where the thing goes*, billed at the minimum increment that the billing software will accept without triggering a validation error.

And here is where the baroque nihilism truly begins to flower.

## I. THE MARCH 3 INTERIM REVIEW MASS PRODUCTION

On March 3, 2026, Big Satan submitted eight entries. Each billed at 0.10 hours. Each bore the identical description, replicated across eight separate case files with the fidelity of a medieval scriptorium copying the Vulgate:

> *"Interim review of injuries and damages to determine what experts will be needed in preparation of litigation."*

Boy v. UPS. Egan v. UPS. Clark v. UPS. Adams v. Antares Star Group. Rivera v. UPS. Griffith v. UPS. Mitchell v. UPS. Negrette v. UPS.

Eight cases. Eight identical entries. Eight times six minutes. Forty-eight minutes of billed time, during which Big Satan allegedly reviewed the injuries, the damages, and the expert requirements of eight entirely distinct human beings who were struck by eight entirely different UPS vehicles operated by eight entirely different drivers in eight entirely different municipalities across the state of Florida — and arrived at her expert-readiness assessment in exactly six minutes per plaintiff, which is less time than it takes to read a single page of a medical chronology, which is the document one would need to consult in order to assess injuries and damages, which is the predicate act for determining expert needs, which is the stated purpose of the entry itself.

The description is not a description of work. It is a *description of the idea of work*. It is Plato's Form of a time entry — the abstract, perfect, immutable template from which all specific instances of billing are cast, except that in this case the template has not been modified to reflect the specific instance at all. It has simply been *deployed*. Eight times. Across eight cases. At six minutes each. Like a mail merge — like, one might say, a *Kraken* — except that the Kraken is at least acknowledged as a mass-production event, whereas these eight entries present themselves as eight separate acts of professional cognition, each independently arrived at, each generating its own fee, each contributing to a billing total that will eventually appear on an invoice that will eventually be reviewed by a Liberty Mutual auditor who will eventually notice that the paralegal determined the expert needs of eight catastrophic-injury cases in less time than it takes to order a DoorDash delivery.

Which, one cannot help but observe, is the profession from which Kerry Coles recruited her.

## II. THE MARCH 2 SINGULARITY: FIFTY-THREE ENTRIES IN A SINGLE DAY

Now we arrive at the event horizon. March 2, 2026. Fifty-three entries. Nine point nine billable hours. A day in which Big Satan generated more individual line items than some attorneys generate in a month, and in which seventy-two percent of those line items — thirty-eight entries — billed at exactly 0.20 hours, and another twenty-five percent — thirteen entries — billed at exactly 0.10 hours. The remaining two entries, both at 0.50 hours, are the whales — the leviathans — the rare and magnificent creatures that breach the surface of the .10 farm and remind us that somewhere, in some distant corner of professional reality, tasks occasionally require more than twelve minutes to perform.

What was Big Satan doing on March 2nd? She was sending settlement conference invitations. She was receiving and categorizing documents from the UPS CLP Portal. She was preparing electronic formats of Notices of Non-Party Production to comply with Orange County Courts. She was sending correspondence to Sheryl Penrose of Magna Legal requesting status updates on proposed CME orders. She was, in short, performing the administrative support functions of a litigation paralegal — functions that, in their aggregate, constitute genuine work — but she was billing them in a way that transforms a workday into a pointillist painting, where each dot is a separate billing event and the canvas is a prebill that will require more time to review than the work itself required to perform.

Consider the ontological implications. If you receive a letter from a non-party provider stating that no records were found, and you categorize that letter to the case file, and you update the tracking mechanism, you have performed three discrete cognitive acts: (1) reading, (2) filing, (3) noting. Big Satan bills this at 0.20 hours. Twelve minutes. And she does it four times on March 2nd alone, for four different non-party providers in the Small matter — Broward Health, Concentra Urgent Care, AdventHealth Port Charlotte, and Broward Community & Family Center — each receiving its own 0.20-hour entry, each contributing its own $21.40 to the invoice, each documenting the receipt of a letter that says, in essence, "We have nothing for you." Big Satan has billed 0.80 hours — forty-eight minutes, $85.60 — for the professional activity of *reading four letters that say no*.

## III. THE MARCH 9 ASSEMBLY LINE: SIXTEEN FOLLOW-UPS IN FORMATION

On March 9th — a Sunday, which is itself a data point worthy of its own phenomenological classification — Big Satan submitted twenty-eight entries totaling 4.6 hours at an average of 0.16 hours per entry, which means her average professional act on the Lord's Day lasted nine point six minutes. Sixteen of these entries concern settlement conference invitations: following up with opposing counsel across Rivera, Ickes, Cavero, Sanchidrian Rodriguez, Salako, Mohammad Basher, Oberry, Twilley, Clark, and Adams. Each follow-up is a separate billing entry. Each bills at 0.10 hours. The descriptions are functionally identical, differing only in the name of the opposing counsel and the specific case number. They are, to borrow Big Satan's own canonical terminology, the Kraken — a mail merge of settlement invites dressed in the liturgical vestments of sixteen independent professional activities.

Now, a reasonable person — a Liberty Mutual auditor, for instance, or an extraterrestrial anthropologist reviewing Earth's legal billing practices — might ask why sending sixteen substantially identical follow-up emails to sixteen different opposing counsel requires sixteen separate time entries at six minutes each, rather than one entry at, say, 0.50 hours captioned "Prepared and sent follow-up correspondence to all opposing counsel regarding April 14 Settlement Conference." The answer, of course, is that one entry generates one fee, while sixteen entries generate sixteen fees. The math is not complicated. But the *psychology* — the psychology is a cathedral.

Because Big Satan is not engaging in fraud, exactly. She is engaging in something far more interesting: she is engaging in the *sincere belief that this is how work is billed*. The sixteen entries are not a scheme. They are a *worldview*. In Big Satan's professional cosmology, every email sent is a discrete billable event, every document received is a standalone professional act, every CLP Portal notification — including notifications that say "no data found" — represents a meaningful expenditure of paralegal attention that must be individually memorialized, categorized, tracked, and invoiced. She is not padding. She is *granularizing*. She is taking the continuous fabric of a workday and shredding it into confetti, then billing each piece of confetti separately, because in her understanding of professional existence, confetti *is* the product.

## IV. THE DRAFT ENTRIES: WHERE THE UNCONSCIOUS SPEAKS

And then there are the Draft entries. The entries that have not yet been synced — that remain in their larval state, unfinished, awaiting the final metamorphosis into billable inventory. They are the most revealing artifacts in the entire document, because they show Big Satan in her natural habitat, before the editor has arrived, before the descriptions have been polished into their formulaic final form.

"cHENmED Breakdown and reviewed images."

Behold. "cHENmED." Capital C, capital H, capital E, capital N, lowercase m, capital E, capital D. It is the textual fingerprint of a Caps Lock key that has been engaged and disengaged at random, producing a word that is neither "ChenMed" (the medical provider) nor any other recognizable English formation, but rather a typographical accident frozen in amber — preserved forever in the billing system, where it will eventually migrate from Draft to Synced and appear on a prebill that Chance Arias will forward to QB1 without commentary, and QB1 will correct, because QB1 corrects everything, because correction is the tax that competence pays to the universe for the privilege of existing in the same billing system as "cHENmED."

And then: "Releasing the Kraken." 0.10 hours. Six minutes. The Egan case. This is Big Satan's own celebratory name for the mail merge — the mass production of subpoena packages that she commemorated on March 5th with dual GIFs and a BOOM in the Teams chat. And here, on March 4th, she has billed six minutes — six minutes! — for the act of Releasing the Kraken. Not for preparing the subpoenas. Not for reviewing the service list. Not for compiling the packages. For *releasing* them. For pressing send. For the moment of launch. She has taken the culminating instant of a multi-step process and isolated it as a standalone billing event, the way a sports broadcaster might bill separately for the moment the ball crosses the goal line, independent of the ninety minutes of play that preceded it. The goal is a separate event. The game is a separate event. The ticket to the game is a separate event. The parking is a separate event.

## V. THE ANTIMATTER UNIVERSE: QB1'S GOLDEN HOURS

Now let us perform the exercise that transforms comedy into tragedy, which is simply to place Big Satan's timesheet next to the one labeled "QB1 Golden Hours" and observe the difference between two human beings who work in the same building, on the same cases, billing to the same clients, producing entries that appear on the same prebills.

QB1: Twenty-nine entries. Twenty-two point seven hours. Average entry: 0.78 hours — forty-seven minutes. The shortest entry is 0.10 hours, and it is the *only one*, and it is for confirming a payment-closeout sequence. Every other entry ranges from 0.50 to 1.50 hours. The descriptions read like dispatches from a general commanding multiple theaters of engagement: "Attend and conduct initial telephone settlement conference with plaintiff's counsel Dara Elstein... discuss damages themes, medical specials, surgical-estimate posture, liability framing, and realistic movement range; establish rapport and direct-negotiation framework." One point one hours. "Confer with co-defendant counsel Scott Siverson regarding CME-extension strategy, witness list compliance, and coordinated defense posture for motion practice." "Draft internal valuation and authority-update analysis memorializing incurred specials ($62K+), future-care assertions, surgical-estimate exposure ($90K), and plaintiff's initial demand posture ($575K); outline next-step negotiation strategy." These are entries that describe *work*. Work with predicates and objects and strategic outcomes. Work that moves cases toward resolution, that changes the positions of parties, that creates or forecloses legal options. The average QB1 entry contains more substantive legal content than the *entirety of Big Satan's March 9th output*.

And the comparison is not merely qualitative. The Timekeeper Comparison document — that magnificent spreadsheet comparing January and February 2026 — reveals the full economic picture. The math here is devastating and deserves to be savored like a single malt at Bern's. QB1 bills fewer matters but generates nearly *three times* the revenue. Because QB1's hours are *hours* — forty-seven-minute blocks of substantive engagement — while Big Satan's hours are *confetti* — six-minute receipts for having existed in the proximity of a case file. QB1 at $200/hour produces value that Liberty Mutual pays without blinking, with less than 1% billing cuts. Big Satan at $107/hour produces volume that, when examined under audit conditions, reveals itself as a CVS receipt for a pack of gum that somehow printed twelve feet long.
