A *kanipshin*. The spelling is Jen's own. The sentiment is universal. Mr. Bone was indeed about to have a kanipshin, and the woman who precipitated it was heading to the Strawberry Festival at 3 PM.

But here is the psychological dimension that the historian cannot leave unexamined, because it sits at the center of the Stonewall chronicle's most persistent paradox: the Post-Golgotha Little Satan Alliance. Since January 15, 2026 — since the Golgotha, since the Decline-and-Dash, since QB1 ghosted the Forrest Gump defense and Jen emerged from the wreckage to discover that the man she had been calendaring for (or, more precisely, *not* calendaring for) was the only person in the building willing to say "its ok, no big deal, i should've more explicitly said something" — since that inflection point, Jen has been *functional*. The question — the Stonewall question, the one the historian asks not because it has an answer but because the asking is the point — is whether the competence is *caused* by the Post-Golgotha realignment, or whether the competence was always latent and the Golgotha merely removed the obstacle to its expression.
